Title: Cybercrime Case Study – Phishing for Professional Credentials

1. Description of the Incident:
Phishing remains one of the most common cybercrimes targeting university students. In this scenario, an attacker impersonates a reputable organization or university department to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information.

2. Real-world Scenario:
A first-year B.Tech student receives an urgent email with a subject line: "Urgent: Internship Offer Selection - Action Required Within 6 Hours." The email uses the official logo of a well-known tech firm and claims the student has been "pre-selected" for a summer internship. To "confirm" the seat, the student is asked to click a link to a portal that looks identical to a university login page. Once the student enters their credentials and UPI details for a "refundable registration fee," the attacker gains full access to their accounts.

3. Target Audience:
This attack specifically targets college freshmen who are actively building their professional profiles on platforms like LinkedIn and are eager for early career opportunities.

4. Consequences:
The immediate consequences include financial loss via unauthorized UPI transactions and a compromised "digital footprint." Furthermore, the attacker can use the student's academic email to send similar phishing links to other batchmates, spreading the "virus" of the scam across the university network.
