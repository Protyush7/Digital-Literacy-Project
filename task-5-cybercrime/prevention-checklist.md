Title: 8-Point Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

1. Examine the Sender: Always verify the actual email address, not just the display name. Look for subtle typos (e.g., support@vltbhopal.ac.in instead of @vitbhopal.ac.in).

2. The "Urgency" Red Flag: Be extremely wary of any communication that demands immediate financial or data-sharing action within a few hours.

3. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Enable 2FA on all primary accounts (Gmail, GitHub, LinkedIn). This ensures that even if a password is stolen, the account remains secure.

4. UPI PIN Safety: Remember that a UPI PIN is only required to send money. If someone asks for a PIN to receive an internship stipend or reward, it is a scam.

5. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Never log into bank portals or academic accounts using unsecured public Wi-Fi networks in cafes or transit hubs.

6. Verify via Official Channels: If an offer seems "too good to be true," verify it by contacting the company directly through their official website or the university placement cell.

7. Use the 1930 Helpline: If you suspect you have been a victim of financial fraud, immediately call 1930, the official National Cyber Crime Helpline of India.

8. Report to Portals: Report any phishing attempts or cybercrimes at the official government portal: cybercrime.gov.in.
